Access to Research Computing compute resources is moderated using finite time-based allocations. Each jobs run in the Research Computing queueing system is assocaited with and charged to an allocation. If the job's allocation is not sufficient for the estimated requirements of the job, the job will not start and additional time must be allocated. (Jobs prevented from running due to an exhausted allocation will be identified with the pending reason "AssociationJobLimit.")

The Research Computing allocation polities and procedures are intended to ensure that the Janus facility and other RC resources are fairly shared, effectively used, and support CU-Boulderâ€™s research programs.

Compute allocations are expressed in SU, with 1 SU equivalent to 1 core-hour on any allocated RC resource. Allocations are granted for 3-12 months, after which they expire.

Use of any Research Computing allocation is subject to the following conditions:

  1. Use of the Janus supercomputer must be acknowledged in publications using the following text:

    This work utilized the Janus supercomputer, which is supported by the National Science Foundation (award number CNS-0821794), the University of Colorado Boulder, the University of Colorado Denver, and the National Center for Atmospheric Research. The Janus supercomputer is operated by the University of Colorado Boulder.

  2. Use of the Janus supercomputer is subject to export control regulations. The PI must cerfity that
    1. The system will be used for fundamental research intended to be published without any restrictions.
    2. The PI will ensure that all users added under their allocation to the system are (a) employees, students, or visitors of a U.S. university, or (b) citizens or permanent residents of the United States.
    3. The PI will retain records or correspondence documenting the export control status of every user requested.
  3. The PI agrees to provide summaries of activities and accomplishments resulting from use of the Janus system to facilitate the annual reporting requirements to the NSF and the VCR office.

Getting a new allocation

All users are allowed a single "startup" or "testing" allocation of 50,000 SU. This amount of time is generally enough for a lot of testing and can be used to develop a proof of concept for a larger allocation request. Use the research computing allocation request form to request a startup allocation. Startup allocations cannot be transferred or shared.

Larger "project" allocations require supporting documentation which may be submitted by the project's principal investigator (PI) or an authorized collaborator. In either case, the PI must have a Research Computing account in order to be associated with the allocation.

Any CU-Boulder researcher, educator, or postdoctoral fellow may request an allocation as a PI for the purpose of research or education. After the allocation has been granted, the project PI may request access for other users, including students and collaborators both local and external. (The project PI is responsible for ensuring that work done by remote collaborators is directly related to the allocated project.

When filling out the allocation request form, be sure to include a proposal that describes

  • the purpose and significance of the project
  • numerical techniques or third-party software that will be used
  • the characteristics of your results and how they will be analyzed and prsented
  • previous work that informs the request, especially any previous use of Research Computing resources
  • any previous publications resulting from the project, especially those that used Research Computing resources
  • how the size of the request was determined
  • alternative implementations that might require more or less resources
  • how your code will use parallel resources, including multi-core CPUs, multi-node jobs, and parallel I/O
  • any work already done to optimize relevant code and workflow (e.g., compiler optimizations, workflow optimizations to more fully utilize nodes)
  • the expected characteristics (CPU, memory, disk) and quantity of your compute jobs, and what sort of monthly load we should expect (i.e., Will the compute time be used roughly equally each month, or 6 months of heavy usage and then a small amount of post-processing work?)
  • funds that support the project, including other compute resources, as well as any efforts for or expectations of future funding
  • results, publications, degrees, or presentations that will be produced using the allocation
  • whether the project is expected to continue beyond this (one-year) allocation

Joining an existing allocation

If you are working on a project that already has an allocation, ask the principal investigator (PI) of the project or allocation to contact rc-help@colorado.edu. The request should include

  • the allocation account ID (usually starting with UCB)
  • the name and username of the person to be added to the allocation

Submitting a job to use an allocation

Jobs submitted to the Research Computing queueing system will automatically use your default allocation. You can explicitely specify a different allocation using the --account (or -A) argument.

$ sbatch --account UCB7111101 job-script.sh

The allocation can be encoded in the job script itself using an #SBATCH directive.

#SBATCH --account UCB7111101

Default allocation

Users with multiple allocations can see their default allocation using the sacctmgr command.

$ sacctmgr show user $USER --parsable
User|Def Acct|Admin|
ralphie|ucb00000055|None|

To change your default allocation, contact rc-help@colorado.edu.

Checking your allocation
