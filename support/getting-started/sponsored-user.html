People collaborating with CU-Boulder researchers may access RC resources, but must first obtain a CU-Boulder sponsored affiliate account. After that point, the process of getting an RC account is the same as for an internal CU-Boulder user.

Get a CU-Boulder sponsored affiliate account

The CU-Boulder faculty or staff member that you are collaborating with must apply for your sponsored account as your account sponsor. The application process is detailed on the CU OIT website; but is as simple as sending the required information to help@colorado.edu.

When your account request has been processed and approved, you will receive login details for your account, including your new CU username and IdentiKey password. You will use these credentials when applying for your RC account.

Complete the RC account request form

Visit the RC Account Request portal to complete the account reqeust form. Select "University of Colorado - Boulder" as your affiliation, and provide your CU IdentiKey credentials (i.e., your username and password) to complete the rest of the form. After review, your account will be created by a member of the Research Computing staff, and login details will be provided to you by email.

Get and register an authenticator

Access to Research Computing resources requires two-factor authentication. Request your physical one-time-password (OTP) generator device by either

  • filling out the OIT Authenticator order form, or
  • emailing your request to rc-help@colorado.edu.

Be prepared to show a state-issued ID when you pick up your authenticator.

Next steps

Now that you have an RC account, you're ready to get started with the specific RC resources that you're interested in using.

I want to run compute applications on the shared infrastructure.
The Janus HPC cluster, Crestone bladecenter, GP-GPU nodes, large-memory nodes, and other compute resources are available to the RC community.
I want to use dedicated nodes in the Research Computing condo.
Dedicated nodes may be purchased as part of the Blanca condo cluster.
I want to store or access data in the PetaLibrary.
The PetaLibrary Active and PetaLibrary Archive storage services can be used to store data for compute or long-term storage.
