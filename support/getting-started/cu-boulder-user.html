Users with an existing CU-Boulder account can request a Research Computing account using the RC Account Request portal, and will authenticate to the RC environment using a Digipass one-time password authenticator.

Complete the account request form

Visit the RC Account Request portal to complete the account reqeust form. Select "University of Colorado - Boulder" as your affiliation, and authenticate using your CU IdentiKey credentials (i.e., your username and password) to complete your account request. After review, your account will be created by a member of the Research Computing staff, and login details will be provided to you by email.

Get and register an authenticator

Access to Research Computing resources requires two-factor authentication. Request your physical one-time-password (OTP) generator device by either

  • filling out the OIT Authenticator order form, or
  • emailing your request to rc-help@colorado.edu.

Be prepared to show your BuffOne card or other state-issued ID when you pick up your authenticator.

Next steps

Now that you have an RC account, you're ready to get started with the specific RC resources that you're interested in using.

I want to run compute applications on the shared infrastructure.
The Janus HPC cluster, Crestone bladecenter, GP-GPU nodes, large-memory nodes, and other compute resources are available to the RC community.
I want to use dedicated nodes in the Research Computing condo.
Dedicated nodes may be purchased as part of the Blanca condo cluster.
I want to store or access data in the PetaLibrary.
The PetaLibrary Active and PetaLibrary Archive storage services can be used to store data for compute or long-term storage.
